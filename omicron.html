Its origins can be traced back to the Phoenician letter "Ayin," which represented an eye. The Greeks adopted Omicron around the 8th century BCE, modifying its shape and sound to fit their language.

In ancient Greece, Omicron was significant in both language and culture. It was not just a letter; it represented a short vowel sound that was essential for pronunciation. Omicron is often associated with clarity and simplicity in communication, making it an important part of the Greek language.
