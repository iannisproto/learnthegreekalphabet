Αα άλφα / alpha /ˈlæm.də/

The letter Lambda is the 11th character of the Greek alphabet. It comes from the Phoenician letter Lamed, a symbol linked to the concept of a goad or a tool used to guide livestock. Early Greek writers adopted Lambda with a simple form that suited the language’s needs. Greek scribes used it in everyday inscriptions and in formal writing, and the character’s shape was refined over time for clarity and ease of use.

In early examples of Greek writing, Lambda was drawn as a simple angled line. Over time, regional variations existed, but the need for clear communication led to a more uniform shape. The letter was also used in the Greek numeral system to represent the number 30 in some contexts. This numerical role helped scholars and traders manage numbers directly in their texts.

Today, Lambda appears in modern Greek writing and in academic subjects such as mathematics and science. In physics, Lambda is often used to denote wavelength, while in computer science, it represents anonymous functions in various programming languages. These uses show that Lambda has maintained its practical role in written communication and technical notation.
