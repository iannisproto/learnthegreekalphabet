Ηη ήτα / eta /ˈiː.tə/

The letter eta is the seventh symbol in the Greek alphabet. In ancient Greek, eta marked a long vowel sound. Over time, its sound shifted. Modern Greek speakers now use eta to produce a sound close to the English long "e." This change shows how language evolves while holding on to its roots.

Scholars study eta to learn about Greek history and literature. Ancient texts use eta in ways that differ from its modern use. This evolution helps experts trace shifts in language over centuries.

Eta also plays a role in science and maths. Statisticians use eta to indicate the strength of a relationship between variables. In physics, eta appears in formulas that describe efficiency and fluid flow. Engineers sometimes choose eta to denote the efficiency of a system.

The form of eta is distinct. It has a simple design that typographers value. The letter’s shape appears in many fonts and styles. Its balanced curves and clear structure add to its appeal.
