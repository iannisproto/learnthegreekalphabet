Δδ δέλτα / delta /ˈdel.tə/

The letter Delta is the fourth letter of the Greek alphabet, represented by the capital "Δ" and lowercase "δ." Its origins can be traced back to the Phoenician letter "Dalet," which meant "door." The Greeks adopted Delta around the 8th century BCE, modifying its shape and sound to fit their language.

In ancient Greece, Delta held significant cultural and linguistic importance. It was not just a letter, it symbolised change and transition. In mathematics and science Delta is often used to represent change or difference, such as in the term "delta" for differences in values. This association with change has made Delta a powerful symbol in various fields, particularly in calculus and physics.

Today, Delta continues to be relevant in many areas. In the Greek numeral system, it represents the number four, being its position in the alphabet. In the context of science, Delta is frequently used in equations as variations, such as in the change of temperature or pressure. This usage proves its value in scientific discussions.

Beyond its mathematical and scientific applications, Delta has also made its way into popular culture. It appears in various names, organisations, and brands, often suggesting transformation or progress. For example, Delta Airlines uses the letter in its name, symbolising movement and travel.
